About

Measurement science for the AI era

I am a psychometrician with over 15 years of operational and research experience in large-scale assessment. I hold a Ph.D. and M.A. in Psychometrics and Quantitative Psychology from Fordham University, and an M.S. in Applied and Mathematical Statistics and a B.A. in Statistics and Psychology from Rutgers University. My career spans research and faculty roles at ETS and the University of Texas at Austin, and a U.S. Department of Education–funded postdoctoral fellowship in advanced quantitative methods at Carnegie Mellon University and RAND.

I am currently a Research Scientist at the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), where I work on measurement and evaluation methods for AI systems. I am also the founder of BroadMetrics, bridging academic psychometrics and real-world applications — from human rating systems and automated scoring evaluation to psychometric approaches for AI benchmarking. I serve as an Associate Editor of Educational Measurement: Issues and Practice and write about measurement and AI evaluation on my Substack, Measuring AI.

Research

Making measurement reliable, interpretable, defensible

θ

Psychometrics for AI Evaluation

Applying validity theory, evidence-centered design, and item response theory to AI benchmarking and evaluation pipelines. AI evaluation faces the same threats to validity that educational measurement solved decades ago — construct underrepresentation, score inflation, benchmark saturation — and those solutions adapt to LLM contexts.

ρ

Raters, Ratings & Human Judgment

Modeling rater effects with the hierarchical rater model (HRM) framework and its extensions. Human judgments — scoring student essays or labeling AI outputs — are measurements subject to systematic error, and psychometric rater models improve the reliability of conclusions drawn from them.

κ

Scoring with Humans & Machines

Methods for evaluating automated scoring engines, including fairness testing, agreement metrics (and the limitations of quadratic weighted kappa), and validity arguments for constructed-response scoring with generative AI.

ψ

Scale Development & Validation

Developing and validating psychometric instruments, from social-emotional learning assessments to new scales for measuring AI behavior, including sycophancy and user metacognition.
